## What This Table Contains

The **cleaned, unified** cost-side view of every U.S. college and university FutureProof knows about. Silver's single job on this table is to take the four pairs of mutually-exclusive Bronze columns (public vs private cost of attendance; public vs private net price; five public quintiles vs five private quintiles) and collapse them into one authoritative column per concept:

- `cost_of_attendance_annual` — sticker price, public or private, whichever this school reports
- `net_price_annual` — real price after aid, routed via `institution_control`
- `net_price_q1` … `net_price_q5` — same, broken out by family income bracket
- `cost_of_attendance_4yr` and `net_price_4yr` — pre-materialized 4-year totals (annual × 4) so the Gold ROI query doesn't have to multiply in the hot path

The table exists so the Gold zone can compute the rewritten ROI formula:

```
ROI = earnings / (net_price_annual × 4 × loan_pct)
```

without having to decide public-vs-private at query time. Silver has already made that decision using `institution_control`.

**Grain:** one row per institution. `unitid` is the natural key; `record_id` (a deterministic SHA-256 hash of `unitid` prefixed `csi-`) is the surrogate.

**CDE density:** 26 of 35 columns are flagged CDE (74.3%) — the 9 unified derived fields plus the 14 raw Bronze pass-throughs preserved for audit reconstruction, plus `record_id`, `unitid`, and `institution_control`.

**PII:** None. All fields are institution-level aggregates from a public federal dataset. No individual student data.

**Coverage reality check:** 2,233 of 3,039 rows (73.48%) have both `net_price_annual` and `cost_of_attendance_annual` populated. The 806 institutions without are concentrated in `PREDDEG=0`/`PREDDEG=4` schools that entered the scope via `ICLEVEL=1`. Downstream consumers must expect nulls on both fields for roughly one in four rows.

### Cost of Attendance (BT-110)

The unified "sticker price" — what a year of attendance nominally costs before financial aid. Includes tuition, fees, books, supplies, room & board, and living expenses.

| Field | Type | Required | CDE | Business Term | Plain-English Definition |
|-------|------|----------|-----|---------------|--------------------------|
| `cost_of_attendance_annual` | double | No | **Yes** | [BT-110](../business-glossary.json) | Average total cost of attendance for one year. Derived as `COALESCE(costt4_a, costt4_p)` — prefers the academic-year figure (~72% of rows), falls back to the program-year figure (~1.3%). Null when both Bronze sources are null (26.5% of institutions). Range-validated to [$5,000, $100,000] at P0. Observed range $6,362–$87,804, median $30,354. |
| `cost_of_attendance_4yr` | double | No | **Yes** | [BT-110](../business-glossary.json) | Pre-materialized 4-year total: `cost_of_attendance_annual × 4`. Null when the annual value is null (null-propagating). IEEE-754 exactness is enforced by SLV-CSI-009 (no floating-point drift tolerated beyond $0.01). Exists as a stored column so the Gold ROI query doesn't recompute `× 4` on every row. |

### Net Price (BT-111)

The unified "real price" — what the average student actually pays after subtracting grants and scholarships from the cost of attendance. **This is the ROI denominator input** for the new formula.

| Field | Type | Required | CDE | Business Term | Plain-English Definition |
|-------|------|----------|-----|---------------|--------------------------|
| `net_price_annual` | double | No | **Yes** | [BT-111](../business-glossary.json) | **CRITICAL CDE.** Average net price for one year at this institution, unified across public and private. Derived via `npt4_pub if institution_control = 'Public' else npt4_priv` — never reads both columns. Can be negative when average grant aid exceeds total cost (community colleges, elite-endowment privates at low-income brackets). Range-validated to [-$5,000, $80,000] at P1. Invariant: `net_price_annual <= cost_of_attendance_annual` (SLV-CSI-007, zero tolerance). Observed range -$1,180 to $77,180, median $18,990. Populated in 73.48% of rows (same rows as COA). |
| `net_price_4yr` | double | No | **Yes** | [BT-111](../business-glossary.json) | **CRITICAL CDE.** Pre-materialized 4-year total: `net_price_annual × 4`. Directly consumed by the ROI formula `earnings / (net_price × 4 × loan_pct)`. Null-propagating. IEEE-754 exactness enforced by SLV-CSI-008. Pre-materialized to eliminate a runtime multiplication in the hot path of the stat engine. |

## Caveats for Consumers

1. **`institution_control` has already done the routing — do not re-derive.** At Silver the multiplexer has already picked the correct column for every unified field. Gold consumers should read `net_price_annual` / `net_price_q1..q5` directly. The `*_pub_raw` and `*_priv_raw` columns are provenance-only — reading them and re-routing would reproduce work and risk inconsistency.

2. **Negative net prices are real.** Three community colleges (Skyline -$1,180, San Diego Mesa -$904, St Petersburg -$52) and several private quintile values report negative net prices because average grant aid exceeds COA. The range validator (SLV-CSI-016) allows down to -$5,000. Downstream consumers must not clip to $0.

3. **Adjacent quintile ordering is not monotonic.** Only the full-span invariant `net_price_q1 <= net_price_q5` is enforced (SLV-CSI-015, ≤50 violations), because 37.9% of private institutions have legitimate Q1 > Q2 inversions from merit-aid targeting. Never assume `q1 ≤ q2 ≤ q3 ≤ q4 ≤ q5`.

4. **Coverage is 73.48%, not 100%.** 806 institutions have null `net_price_annual` **and** null `cost_of_attendance_annual` (the two are row-identical in nullness — Key Finding #1 from the EDA). These rows will still flow through the Silver→Gold LEFT JOIN, but the cost fields will be null. Downstream UIs must handle this case gracefully.

5. **Quintile coverage degrades as income rises.** `net_price_q1` is 71.90% populated; `net_price_q5` is 60.74%. The high-income quintile is suppressed more often because samples are smaller. Per-quintile thresholds (not a single "all quintiles ≥ X%" rule) are therefore what the DQ pack enforces for the ROI-critical `q1`/`q5` span check.

6. **For-profit coverage is the weak spot.** Only 52.63% of `institution_control='Private for-profit'` rows have `net_price_annual`. This is a source-side IPEDS underreporting pattern, tracked but not blocked (SLV-CSI-014 is P2 informational). Downstream consumers should hedge confidence on for-profit rows.

7. **Tuition is NOT the ROI denominator.** `tuition_in_state` / `tuition_out_of_state` are tuition-only, not net of aid. The ROI formula uses `net_price_annual`. Confusing the two materially misstates student cost.

8. **`state_abbr` does NOT join to BEA RPP.** The RPP purchasing-power adjustment is keyed on the student-selected destination state, not the institution's home state. `state_abbr` is a display/filter field only.

9. **The `*_raw` provenance columns are null on the non-matching control branch.** A public school's row will have `npt4_pub_raw` populated and `npt4_priv_raw` null, and vice versa. This is correct — no cross-contamination — but means the raw fields are not useful for recomputing anything; use the unified derived fields instead.
