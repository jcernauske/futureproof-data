About the School
Illinois State University

Illinois State University is a public research institution in Normal, Illinois, founded in 1857 as the state's first public university. With roughly 21,000 students, it sits in the mid-size sweet spot — large enough for serious research resources and industry connections, small enough that you're not lost in a lecture hall of 800.

The Computer Science program lives in the School of Information Technology, which has steadily expanded its faculty and lab space over the past decade. ISU grads in CS benefit from strong regional hiring pipelines — State Farm, Rivian, and Caterpillar all recruit heavily from this campus. The Bloomington-Normal metro also has a cost of living well below the national average, which matters when you're weighing debt against starting salary.
