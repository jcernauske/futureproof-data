Zone 1: Identity

Emotional anchor. The avatar badge sits in a 48px rounded square with a build-colored gradient background, centered above the school name. School name is 18px Fredoka bold. Major is 13px secondary. Character name and career share a single 11px Space Mono line in the build's accent color. This zone answers: "Who am I in this future?"

Zone 2: Stats

Data core. Five stat bars at 6px height (up from 2px) for much stronger scannability across columns. Stat labels are 11px Space Mono, values are 13px bold in the stat's own color. The 10px gap between rows keeps them breathable but dense enough to compare across all four cards without your eye losing alignment.

Zone 3: Cost

The punchline. Net price in 20px bold Space Mono anchors the bottom of every card. The "/yr" suffix is 12px muted so it doesn't compete with the number itself. "NET PRICE" label below in 10px uppercase tracking provides context without visual weight. This is the number they'll screenshot and send to their parents.

Design Rationale

Why Three Zones?

The core problem with compare cards is information hierarchy at small widths. When everything flows as a single visual stream, your eye has to hunt for what matters. By splitting into three bordered zones, we create a scannable rhythm: your eye can sweep horizontally across all four cards at the same vertical position and compare the same data type instantly. The borders aren't decorative -- they're wayfinding.

Why 6px Stat Bars?

At 2px, bars become decorative hairlines. You can tell they exist, but you can't compare relative lengths at a glance across four cards. At 6px, each bar has enough visual mass that your peripheral vision can register differences without focusing. The subtle inner highlight (rgba(255,255,255,0.2) gradient from top) adds depth without increasing visual weight -- it reads as dimensionality, like a glass tube.

Why the Left Accent Strip?

The 2px gradient strip (build accent fading to transparent at 85%) serves two functions: it color-codes each card to match the build's identity, and it creates a subtle "spine" that grounds the card without a heavy left border. The gradient fade means it doesn't draw your eye to the bottom of the card -- it reinforces the top (identity zone) where the color matters most.

Token References

  • Card background: bg-mid (#232545)
  • Zone dividers: border-subtle (rgba(255,255,255,0.06))
  • Card border: border-default (rgba(255,255,255,0.1))
  • Avatar backgrounds: build accent at 22% / 6% opacity gradient
  • Stat bar tracks: rgba(255,255,255,0.06)
  • Cost price: text-primary (#F5F0E8), font-data (Space Mono)
  • Zone 1 school name: font-display (Fredoka), 18px, weight 700
