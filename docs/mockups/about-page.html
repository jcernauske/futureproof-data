FutureProof reads from public, citable datasets. No black boxes, no scraped LinkedIn profiles, no startup vibes. Eleven receipts and one model. Here they are.

01 — Why this exists

A career-planning tool only works if students trust the math.

Counselors get pitched "AI career tools" every week and most of them can't say where a single number came from. FutureProof was built on the opposite premise: every stat on every screen traces back to a U.S. government dataset or a published academic study. The bear is fun. The numbers are real.

02 — The pipeline

Bronze, Silver, Gold — three zones, one ground truth.

Public data lands as-is in the Bronze zone (raw, untouched, audit-ready). It's normalized into Silver (consistent types, joinable on CIP and SOC). Gold is the small, opinionated set of tables the app actually reads — career outcomes, occupation profiles, regional adjustments. If a Gold number changes, you can walk it back to a Bronze file and a public URL.

Bronze

Raw, as published

Government CSVs, BLS tables, O*NET dumps. Stored verbatim. Nothing edited.

College Scorecard — Field of Study

Earnings one year and four years after graduation, by school × major (CIP code). Powers the Year-1 peer band on every Build Results page.

Some program-school cells are suppressed for privacy when the cohort is too small. We render those as "data unavailable," never as zero.

◆ Data source 2020 rev.

NCES + BLS

CIP–SOC Crosswalk

The bridge between programs (CIP) and occupations (SOC). Without this, "Marketing major" can't connect to "Marketing Manager." It's the seam that makes the whole pipeline work.

A CIP can map to many SOCs and vice-versa. We surface the highest-weighted matches; rare or speculative pairings are filtered.

nces.ed.gov/ipeds/cipcode
◆ Data source 2023
⌬

Open-source · GitHub

Karpathy AI Exposure Scores

Per-occupation exposure to large language model automation, derived from O*NET task descriptions. Powers half of the RES stat and the Fight AI boss difficulty.

Exposure ≠ replacement. A high score means an LLM can plausibly perform the task, not that the job will disappear. We pair it with Anthropic Economic Index for a more honest read.

github.com/karpathy

Anthropic Economic Index

Real-world Claude usage telemetry, mapped to occupations. Tells us where AI is actually being used today, not just where it could be. Tunes the RES stat against measured ground truth.
