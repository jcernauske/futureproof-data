Boss Fight Results

5 fights. Your stats vs. real-world threats.

What changed and why

Result as hero. "VICTORY" / "DEFEATED" / "STANDOFF" in 22px Fredoka bold replaces a 15px Space Mono pill. The result now dominates the right side of every card. You see green/amber/gold before you read anything.

Environmental tinting. Each card's background gradient shifts based on the result. WIN cards fade from boss color to subtle thrive green. LOSE cards carry heavier boss color into amber. The entire card tells you the outcome through color, not just a badge.

Edge accents. A 4px result-colored stripe on the right edge creates a "scoreboard" feel -- like marks on a tally. The boss color stripe on the left identifies the boss. Two stripes, two stories: who you fought, and whether you won.

Fight score bar. A vertical 4px bar shows how close the fight was. This adds data density without text -- you can see at a glance which fights were close calls and which were blowouts.

Boss portrait refinement. The 88px solid-colored rectangle becomes a 64px rounded square with a gradient from boss color at 25% to 12%. Smaller, more refined, with a subtle glow. The emoji sits on top of depth, not a flat slab.

Vocabulary shift. "WIN/LOSE/DRAW" becomes "VICTORY/DEFEATED/STANDOFF." Game language, not spreadsheet language. The flavor text underneath ("Your stats held strong" / "This one got you" / "A narrow escape") adds personality.

Expand/collapse preserved. The banner is always visible; narrative expands below. LOSE and DRAW auto-expand. This matches the spec's information density goals while adding visual drama to the always-visible layer.

CSS custom properties per boss. Each card sets --boss-color-alpha-* variables so the gradient, portrait, and stripe all derive from one source. Clean to implement in React with inline style props.
