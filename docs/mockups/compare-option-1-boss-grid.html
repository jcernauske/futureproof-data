Dense but scannable. Each boss row is ~48px tall. Five rows total = ~240px for the entire grid. The whole compare view (cards + bosses) fits in a single viewport scroll at 1200px wide. No accordion, no tabs, no interaction needed — just scan.

Design Rationale: Continuous Extension

The current implementation has a "Risk Headlines" section header with a divider dot separating the school cards from the boss grid. This creates a visual break that forces the student to re-orient: "ok, new section, what am I looking at now?"

By removing that break and letting the boss grid flow as a natural continuation of the card columns, we get several benefits:

1. Column alignment becomes the organizing principle. Each card's left accent strip color maps directly to its boss result column. No mental re-mapping needed.

2. Reduced cognitive load. There's nothing to "section" — it's all one comparison. Stats above, threats below. Same columns, same builds.

3. The boss grid earns its visual weight through data density, not through a section header announcing it. Five rows of color-coded pills speak for themselves.

4. Vertical space savings of ~60px (header text + divider + padding). On compare, every pixel matters because we want the full picture visible without scrolling.
