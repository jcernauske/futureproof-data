## SCENE 1 — "The Broken Pipeline" (0:08–0:38)

### BEAT 1 (0:08–0:16) — The Cost Is Insane
*[Animated line chart: tuition rising since 1980s]*

VOICEOVER: "College tuition has tripled in the last 40 years. The average degree now costs $31,000 a year at a public school. $65,000 at a private one."

*[Counter animates: $1.8 TRILLION]*

"And we're $1.8 trillion in debt paying for it."

### BEAT 2 (0:16–0:22) — The Loans Are Predatory
*[Rising bar: Parent PLUS borrowing up 750% in 30 years]*

VOICEOVER: "Parent PLUS loans charge 9% interest with no borrowing cap. Six thousand college programs leave graduates owing more than they earn."

### BEAT 3 (0:22–0:28) — Rich Kids Are Fine
*[Split visual: private consultant ($500K) vs. 372:1 counselor ratio]*

VOICEOVER: "If your family can afford a half-million-dollar admissions consultant, you'll be fine. But for the rest of us? One counselor for every 372 students — spending just 19% of their time on college advising."

### BEAT 4 (0:28–0:34) — Vibes Over Outcomes
*[Quick cuts: lazy rivers, stadiums, dining halls, campus tours]*

VOICEOVER: "So kids pick colleges based on vibes — sports teams, cafeterias, dorm rooms. They're not trained to focus on outcomes. And the people with the data to help? They haven't made it easy."

### BEAT 5 (0:34–0:38) — The Pivot
*[Everything freezes. Goes dark. Logo.]*

VOICEOVER: "We built something that does."

## SCENE 4 — "For Everyone" (1:45–2:38)

### BEAT 13 (1:45–2:00) — The Counselor's Problem
*[Split screen: 372:1 ratio stat → counselor at a desk with a line of students out the door]*

VOICEOVER: "A guidance counselor at a public school has 372 students and 19% of their day for college advising. That's roughly 90 seconds per student per session. They don't have time to research every major at every school, cross-reference salary data, run loan projections, and assess AI risk. Nobody does. That's an impossible job."

### BEAT 14 (2:00–2:12) — FutureProof Makes It Possible
*[Screen recording: counselor flow — pull up a student's build, pentagon + boss results on screen]*

VOICEOVER: "FutureProof gives that counselor a fact-based, data-backed conversation in 90 seconds. Pull up a school and major — the stats are there, the risks are there, the tradeoffs are visible. No guessing. No vibes. Just an honest picture they can walk through together."

### BEAT 15 (2:12–2:20) — Multilingual
*[UI switching between English, Spanish, Arabic]*

VOICEOVER: "And when a first-generation student brings in parents who don't speak English, FutureProof meets them where they are — in Spanish and Arabic, the two most common non-English languages spoken in U.S. households with high school students."

### BEAT 16 (2:20–2:30) — The Ollama Moment
*[Terminal: Ollama launching → app running locally]*

VOICEOVER: "It runs on the school's own hardware through Ollama. No cloud. No API costs. No student data leaving the building. Install it once — it's theirs forever."

### BEAT 16b (2:30–2:38) — The Branches
*[Screen recording: branch tree expanding from a single career node]*

VOICEOVER: "And when a student says, 'this is the only job I can get with this degree' — the counselor pulls up the branch tree. One starting position. Dozens of paths forward. Every one backed by data."

## Production Notes

- **Bookend structure:** Jeff on camera opens and closes the video. Open is raw and anxious ("I got anxiety"). Close is resolved and personal ("as a father of three teens, this is what I needed"). Simple setup — no studio, no over-production. Authenticity wins.
- **Stats sources:** All stats from "The Broken College Career Planning Pipeline" research brief. Key sources: College Board Trends 2025, Federal Reserve, BLS, ASCA, NACAC, Chetty/Deming/Friedman (Opportunity Insights).
- **Animated charts (Scene 1):** Canva templates or Flourish for tuition line chart and debt counter. Midjourney for cinematic background frames if time permits.
- **Screen recordings (Scenes 2–4):** Real app, real data, real schools. No mockups.
- **Pentagon montage (Beat 8):** Can be pre-rendered static images with text overlay — no screen recording needed.
- **Ollama beat:** Must show actual terminal output of Gemma running locally. Judges for the Ollama track need to see this is real.
- **Languages implemented:** English, Spanish, Arabic (scoped to top 3 non-English household languages for U.S. high school students).
- **Branches (Beat 16b):** Counselor framing — branches as a tool to expand a student's view, not a tech flex.
- **Pipeline credibility (280+ DQ rules, adversarial auditor, 700K+ rows):** Light touch in video (Beat 6 covers the 700K rows and 5 sources). Full story carried in Kaggle writeup.
