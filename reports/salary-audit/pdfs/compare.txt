GEMMA'S VERDICT
Miami Dade College focuses on getting you into the workforce quickly with very little debt. You optimize for financial safety
because your starting salary for Registered nurses covers your $25,070 in student loans in about one year. The tradeoff is that
the school name carries less weight on a resume and you might hit a career ceiling sooner than in other fields. California
Institute of Technology optimizes for high prestige and a high median salary of $134,830 as an Aerospace engineer. This path
offers a growing job market but requires you to take on a large amount of debt, totaling $167,196. You are trading financial
ease and protection against automation for a powerful brand name and a high-level technical career. Harvard University
optimizes for extreme brand recognition and a very high starting salary of $140,072 for Computer network support
specialists. However, the cost of the degree is very high, leaving you with $165,684 in debt that takes longer to pay back
relative to your earnings. You also face a higher risk of your specific job tasks being changed by AI technology. Your decision
comes down to whether you want to prioritize immediate financial stability or invest heavily in a high-prestige name for
long-term career potential.
